# Lesson 2.1: Ethics, Morals, and Law in ICT Practice

## Lesson Objectives
By the end of this lesson, you should be able to:
* **Define** ethics, morals, and law, and explain how they differ.
* **Analyze** the relationship and tensions between what is legal and what is ethical.
* **Apply** these concepts to ICT practice using a real-life case study.
* **Critically reflect** on how ICT professionals should navigate dilemmas involving competing legal and ethical demands.

---

## 1. Understanding the Key Concepts

### a) Morals
* **Definition:** Personal principles or beliefs about what is right and wrong.
* **Source:** Shaped by culture, religion, upbringing, and personal philosophy.
* **Example:** Personally believing it is wrong to read a colleague's emails, even if you have the administrative access to do so.

### b) Ethics
* **Definition:** Shared standards of behavior within a profession or society.
* **Source:** Professional bodies and agreed-upon frameworks.
* **Example:** Adhering to the ACM or ICTAK codes of conduct that mandate user privacy and data confidentiality.

### c) Law
* **Definition:** Formal rules enacted by governments and enforced by authorities.
* **Source:** Legislation and judicial systems.
* **Example:** **Kenyaâ€™s Data Protection Act 2019**, which regulates how personal data is collected and stored.

## 2. The Relationship Between Ethics and Law

They often overlap, but they are not identical. Understanding the "Grey Areas" is critical for a professional:

* **Legal but Unethical:** Mass surveillance that is technically allowed under a vague security law but violates fundamental human privacy.
* **Ethical but Illegal:** A "whistleblower" who leaks proof of corporate harm. They break a Non-Disclosure Agreement (illegal) to protect the public (ethical).
* **Moral but Unethical:** A "hacktivist" who feels morally justified in crashing a site for a cause, yet violates professional codes regarding system integrity.

## 3. Case Study: The FlexiSpy/DCI Surveillance Incident (2024)

### The Dilemma
* **Legality:** Security agencies may argue surveillance is permitted under national security provisions.
* **Ethics:** Professional codes demand that ICT workers protect privacy and avoid harm. Assisting in targeted surveillance of journalists contradicts these codes.
* **Morals:** Public outcry suggested that many citizens viewed this as fundamentally "wrong," regardless of what the law stated.

## 4. Lessons for ICT Professionals

* **Beyond Compliance:** Ethical responsibility often requires more than just "following the law."
* **Frameworks Matter:** Use codes of ethics (ACM, IEEE, ICTA) as your shield when facing pressure from employers or governments.
* **Long-term Credibility:** Upholding ethics protects public trust in technology, which is more valuable than short-term legal or political protection.

> **Final Thought:** In the digital era, just because we **can** build it or deploy it, doesn't mean we **should**.

The #RejectFinanceBill protests highlighted a major tension: **National Security vs. Individual Privacy.**

* **The Incident:** Protesters reported being tracked via mobile data. In court, evidence emerged of telcos providing location details to security agencies without clear warrants or user consent.

## 3. Everyday Digital Violations

### ðŸ”¹ Recording & Posting Without Consent
Recording a stranger for a "street interview" or capturing someone in the background of your TikTok without permission can be a violation of the DPA. Under Kenyan law, an image of a face is **Personal Data**.

### ðŸ”¹ Posting Minors
Posting children under 18 without parental consent is a direct violation. The Act (and the Children's Act 2022) views the "Best Interest of the Child" as the highest priority.

### ðŸ”¹ Screen Recording Private Chats
Taking a screenshot of a "Once View" photo or a private WhatsApp conversation and sharing it publicly may be considered an unauthorized processing of personal data.

## 4. Legal Landmark: Wanjiru vs. Machakos University (2022)
This case set the precedent for **Image Rights** in Kenya.

**The Case:** Machakos University used a student's graduation photo for a commercial advertisement for computer packages without her consent.
**The Court's Ruling:**
1.  **Violation of Privacy:** The university used her image for financial gain without authority.
2.  **Intellectual Property:** Her "personality rights" (the right to control her likeness) were infringed.
3.  **The Penalty:** The university was ordered to pay **Ksh 700,000** in damages.

> **Key Takeaway:** Your face is your data. Using it for business without your "Yes" is expensive for the offender.

## 5. Your Rights as a Data Subject


1.  **Right to be Informed:** "Why are you taking my photo?"
2.  **Right of Access:** "Show me what data you have on me."
3.  **Right to Erasure:** "Delete my account and all associated data permanently." (The Right to be Forgotten).
4.  **Right to Object:** "Stop sending me promotional SMS every Friday night."

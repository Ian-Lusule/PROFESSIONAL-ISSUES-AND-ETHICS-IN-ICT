## 1. Introduction: Why Data Privacy Matters
In the digital economy, **"Data is the new oil."** It is immensely valuable but highly volatile. 
* **The Risk:** Misuse leads to identity theft, financial loss (e.g., Fuliza fraud), and systemic loss of public trust.
* **The Professional Duty:** ICT workers are the "gatekeepers" of this data. Your technical choices determine whether a system is a fortress or a sieve.

## 3. Case Study I: Worldcoin (The "Eyeball" Scan)
In 2023, **Worldcoin** offered ~Ksh 7,000 in crypto tokens to Kenyans in exchange for an iris scan to create a "World ID."

### The Lapses:
1. **Consent vs. Inducement:** Was consent "freely given" if participants were swayed by a financial reward in a struggling economy?
2. **Missing DPIA:** Worldcoin failed to submit a **Data Protection Impact Assessment** before starting large-scale biometric collection.
3. **Cross-Border Transfer:** Sensitive biometric data was sent outside Kenya without adequate legal safeguards.

**The Outcome:** The High Court ordered the suspension of registrations and the **permanent deletion** of all collected biometric data within 7 days.

## 4. Case Study II: Huduma Namba (NIIMS)
A government initiative to create a central digital ID for all residents.

### The Lapses:
* **Legal Prematurity:** The project was rolled out *before* the Data Protection Act was fully operational.
* **Invasive Data:** Attempted collection of **DNA and GPS** data was ruled "intrusive and unnecessary" by the courts.
* **Exclusion Risk:** Marginalized groups (e.g., Nubians) faced "digital invisibility" if they couldn't meet the strict registration requirements.

**The Outcome:** In October 2021, the High Court declared the rollout **illegal** because the government skipped the mandatory **DPIA** required under Section 31 of the DPA.

---

## 5. The Critical Tool: What is a DPIA?
A **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)** is a systematic process to identify and minimize privacy risks. It is **mandatory** for high-risk projects.



### When is a DPIA required?
* When using **new technologies** (AI, Biometrics).
* When processing data on a **large scale** (National IDs).
* When processing **sensitive data** (Health, Religion, DNA).

---

## 6. Summary for ICT Professionals
* **Legality vs. Ethics:** A project might be "ordered" by a boss or government, but it can still be unethical and illegal if privacy rights are ignored.
* **Foreseeable Risk:** As a professional, you must advocate for a DPIA *before* the first line of code is written or the first user is registered.
* **Societal Impact:** Your work impacts human rights. Safeguarding data is safeguarding people.

# CCS 4204: Professional Issues and Ethics in ICT

## Course Overview
**Prerequisite:** Done at the end of the course with an overall understanding of Computer Science.

**Course Purpose:** This course aims to educate existing and future business managers and ICT professionals on the tremendous impact that ethical issues have on the use of information technology in the modern business world.

---

## Expected Learning Outcomes
By the end of this course unit, students should be able to:

* **Synthesize** and present a resolved project outcome understanding ethical and socio-technical challenges.
* **Evaluate** the role of standards, codes of conduct, and legislative/regulatory obligations in the ICT industry.
* **Review** the roles and responsibilities of ICT professionals regarding work-life balance, mentoring, and life-long learning.
* **Communicate** effectively as a professional and function as a leader or member of a diverse team.

---

## Weekly Schedule

| Week | Topics | Activities / Case Studies |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | Introduction to Professional Issues in ICT | Overview of ICT profession; Codes of conduct (ACM, IEEE, BCS); Kenya’s ICT Authority guidelines. |
| **2** | Ethics, Morality, and Technology | Ethical theories (utilitarianism, deontology); Facebook–Cambridge Analytica scandal. |
| **3** | Privacy, Data Protection, and Surveillance | GDPR & Kenya Data Protection Act 2019; Workplace surveillance; Privacy audits. |
| **4** | Intellectual Property (IP) | Copyrights, patents, trademarks; Software piracy; Oracle vs. Google (Java API). |
| **5** | Cybersecurity and Cybercrime | Ethical hacking; Kenya’s Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act (2018/2024). |
| **6** | AI and Ethical Concerns | Bias, fairness, and transparency; Deepfakes; Future of work and automation. |
| **7** | Emerging Technologies | Cloud, IoT, blockchain, quantum computing; Green computing and sustainability. |
| **8** | Responsibility and Accountability | Whistleblowing; Conflicts of interest; Uber’s data breach case study. |
| **9** | ICT and Society | Digital divide; ICT for sustainable development (SDGs, Vision 2030); Accessibility. |
| **10** | Globalization and Regulation | ICT governance (ICANN, ITU); Global outsourcing and remote work impacts. |
| **11** | Professional Development | Certification (AWS, Cisco, etc.); Soft skills; Industry readiness and mock interviews. |
| **12** | Capstone: Ethical Challenges | Student presentations on real-world ICT projects; Final reflection essays. |

## 1. Introduction: The Value of Intangibles
In the industrial age, wealth was measured by land and factories. In the **Digital Age**, wealth is measured by **Intangible Assets**: code, algorithms, brand reputation, and creative content. 

IP laws serve as a "social contract": they give creators a temporary monopoly over their work to encourage them to keep innovating, but they also ensure that knowledge eventually enters the public domain for the benefit of everyone.

---

## 2. Types of Intellectual Property Rights
Digital creators often use a combination of these rights to protect a single product.



### üñãÔ∏è Copyrights
* **What they protect:** Original creative "expressions" fixed in a tangible medium.
* **Digital Examples:** Source code, website layouts, YouTube videos, and blog posts.
* **Key Rule:** Protection is **automatic** the moment you create the work. In Kenya, this lasts for the life of the author plus **50 years**.

### üí° Patents
* **What they protect:** Functional inventions and technical processes.
* **Digital Examples:** A unique data compression algorithm, a new type of touchscreen hardware, or a specific blockchain protocol.
* **Key Rule:** Patents are not automatic; they must be registered with bodies like **KIPI** (Kenya Industrial Property Institute). They typically last for **20 years**.

### üè∑Ô∏è Trademarks
* **What they protect:** Signs, logos, and names that distinguish a brand.
* **Digital Examples:** The Safaricom "Green" hex code and logo, the Twitter/X icon, or the name "M-PESA."
* **Key Rule:** Trademarks can last **indefinitely** as long as they are renewed every 10 years and remain in use.

## 3. The Open Source Alternative
Not all creators want a monopoly. **Open Source Licenses** (like MIT, Apache, or GPL) allow developers to share their code while still keeping some rights.

* **Opportunities:** Collaboration, transparency, and rapid security patches.
* **Challenges:** "License compliance"‚Äîif you use open-source code in a commercial app, you must follow the specific rules (e.g., giving credit to the original author).

## 4. The AI Challenge: Who Owns the Output?
Generative AI (like ChatGPT or Midjourney) is disrupting traditional IP law. 
* **The Training Issue:** Is it legal for AI companies to "scrape" copyrighted art and books to train their models? 
* **The Authorship Issue:** In most jurisdictions (including Kenya), copyright only applies to works created by a **human**. AI-generated images with zero human input often fall into the **Public Domain**.

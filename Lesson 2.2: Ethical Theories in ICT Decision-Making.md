## âš–ï¸ 2. The Three Pillars of Ethical Theory

### a) Utilitarianism (Consequentialism)
* **Principle:** An action is right if it produces the "greatest good for the greatest number." It focuses entirely on the **outcome**.
* **ICT Implication:** A professional might justify a security patch that slows down a system if it protects 99% of users from a hack.
* **FlexiSpy Case Analysis:**
    * **Pro-Surveillance:** If spying on a few journalists prevents a major national security threat, the "utility" (safety) of the majority outweighs the privacy of the few.
    * **Anti-Surveillance:** The long-term harm to press freedom and public trust creates more "pain" for society than the "pleasure" of the data gathered.

### b) Deontology (Duty-Based Ethics)
* **Principle:** An action is right if it follows universal moral rules and duties, regardless of the consequences.
* **ICT Implication:** If the rule is "Do not lie," a developer must be honest about a system flaw even if it costs the company a million-dollar contract.
* **FlexiSpy Case Analysis:** * Installing spyware without informed consent violates the fundamental **duty to respect privacy**. From a deontological view, this is wrong regardless of whether it catches a criminal.

### c) Virtue Ethics (Character-Based Ethics)
* **Principle:** Ethics is about cultivating virtuous traits like honesty, courage, and integrity. It asks: *"What kind of person/organization do we become if we do this?"*
* **ICT Implication:** A "virtuous" programmer writes clean, documented code not because they are told to, but because they value **excellence** and **accountability**.
* **FlexiSpy Case Analysis:**
    * By using secret spyware, an organization moves away from the virtues of **transparency** and **justice**, instead cultivating a culture of **secrecy** and **deception**.

## 4. Application: Navigating the FlexiSpy Dilemma

When we look at the DCI case through these combined lenses, we see why it is so controversial:

1.  **Utilitarianism** provides the state's potential defense (Safety > Individual Privacy).
2.  **Deontology** provides the professional's defense (Privacy = A Universal Right).
3.  **Virtue Ethics** provides the societal critique (Secrecy = Lack of Integrity).

---

## 5. Summary Reflection
As an ICT professional, you will rarely use just one theory. Most experts use a **pluralistic approach**:
* Use **Deontology** to check if you are breaking any fundamental professional rules.
* Use **Utilitarianism** to see the potential impact on the community.
* Use **Virtue Ethics** to ensure you are becoming the kind of professional the industry respects.

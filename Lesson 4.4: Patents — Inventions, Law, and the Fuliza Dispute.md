## 1. What is a Patent?
A patent is an exclusive right granted for an **invention**â€”a product or a process that provides a new way of doing something or offers a new technical solution to a problem.



### The "Social Bargain" of Patents
* **The Reward:** The state grants the inventor a **20-year monopoly** to exclusively make, use, or sell the invention.
* **The Cost:** The inventor must **publicly disclose** exactly how the invention works so that others can learn from it and build upon it once the patent expires.

---

## 2. The Three Pillars of Patentability
To be granted a patent by the **Kenya Industrial Property Institute (KIPI)**, your invention must meet these criteria:

1.  **Novelty (Newness):** The invention must not have been disclosed to the public anywhere in the world before the filing date. *Tip: Don't demo your tech at a conference before filing!*
2.  **Inventive Step (Non-Obviousness):** The solution must not be obvious to a "person skilled in the art" (e.g., a senior software engineer). It must represent a leap in logic, not just a minor tweak.
3.  **Industrial Applicability:** The invention must be capable of being used in any kind of industry (including agriculture and services).

> **Note:** Under Kenyan law (Section 21 of the IPA), "Methods of doing business" and "Pure mathematical methods" are **not** patentable. This is often where ICT disputes begin.

## 3. Case Study: Akuon v. Safaricom (The "Fuliza" Dispute)
**Case:** *Dr. Peter Odero Akuon v. Safaricom PLC & Others (2020)*

### The Claim
Dr. Akuon claimed that Safaricomâ€™s **Fuliza** (an M-Pesa overdraft facility) infringed on his patent titled *"Mobile Virtual Bank Account Management."* He described a system where users could access virtual accounts and credit via USSD or mobile money without a traditional bank account.

### The Tribunal's Decision
The **Industrial Property Tribunal (IPT)** dismissed the claim and **revoked** Dr. Akuon's patent. 
* **Reason 1: Business Method Exclusion.** The Tribunal ruled the invention was essentially a "scheme for banking services"â€”a method of doing businessâ€”which is excluded from patent protection.
* **Reason 2: Lack of Novelty.** The core elements (pooling funds, mobile interfaces) were already found in "prior art" like M-Shwari (launched 2012) and KCB M-Pesa.
* **Reason 3: Lack of Essential Integers.** Safaricom proved that Fuliza used existing infrastructure that did not replicate the specific technical features claimed in Akuon's patent.

## 4. Ethical & Professional Challenges

| Challenge | Impact on ICT Professionals |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Power Imbalance** | Large corporations have "Patent War Chests" and legal teams that can overwhelm small inventors. |
| **Patent Trolls** | Entities that buy patents just to sue others for "licensing fees" without ever building a product. |
| **Disclosure Fear** | Once you patent, your "secret sauce" is public. Competitors might try to "work around" your patent with minor changes. |
